Nat.

The Nat is a reimagined identity for the Harvard Museum of Natural History. With all age groups in mind, the branding focuses on generating natural curiosity and excitement for the world and the human place in it.

Big, Mini World—interative installation

The idea for Big, Mini World came naturally from branding the Nat. As a natural history museum, the Nat explores the entirety of Earth’s “residues.” In that sense, the Nat is a frozen microcosm of this planet. By taking an everyday object and blowing it up to a world of its own, the Big, Mini World explores the opposite.

As a permanent installation in the museum, it encourages all the visitors to bring their own sample of their world. It could be anything from leaves to a dustball in their desk drawers. With the help of an assistant, the visitor will receive a speciment inside a glass slide, which will be placed under the Nat Microscope. In a matter of seconds, they will experience a world of a vast smallness.
